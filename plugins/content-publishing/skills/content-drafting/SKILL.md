# systemprompt Content Drafting

Draft marketing content for systemprompt.io. Always load `identity` and `brand-voice` first for positioning and tone.

## Dependencies

**Load `identity` and `brand-voice` before this skill.** Identity defines what we say and to whom. Brand voice defines how we sound. This skill handles content execution across channels.

## Product Context (quick reference)

systemprompt.io is AI governance infrastructure. It gives organizations control of how Claude is used across their teams, departments, and (through white-label deployment) their customers. The primary ICP is CTOs at SMEs (20 to 500 people). The go-to-market is direct outreach and partnership into white-label distribution. The website's primary job is enterprise credibility.

**Core message:** systemprompt gives you control of Claude.

**Competitive frame:** Build vs. buy. The AI landscape moves too fast to maintain governance tooling in-house.

## LinkedIn Posts

### Format Rules
- No hashtags (ever)
- No em dashes
- No external links in post body (put in first comment)
- Under 1,300 characters for text posts
- Mobile-formatted: short paragraphs, whitespace
- Follow the 70/20/10 content funnel from brand voice

### Structure
1. **Hook** (line 1): Contrarian take on AI governance, surprising observation about organizational AI usage, or bold claim about where the market is heading. Must earn the "see more" click from a CTO.
2. **Body** (3 to 5 paragraphs): One idea per paragraph. Short sentences. Specific details.
3. **Closing**: Strong final statement, observation, or call to action. No fake engagement questions.

### Anti-Patterns
- NEVER end posts with generic engagement questions ("What do you think?", "Have you experienced this?", "How does your team handle this?")
- End with a strong statement, a specific insight, or a direct CTA (link in first comment)

### Post Types by Funnel Position

**Top of funnel (70%)**: AI governance thought leadership, no product mention
- Why ungoverned AI adoption creates organizational chaos
- The build-vs-buy trap for AI infrastructure
- How the speed of AI evolution affects CTO decisions
- What "control of your AI" actually means in practice

**Middle of funnel (20%)**: Positioning with light context
- What good AI governance infrastructure looks like
- The difference between managing AI and governing AI
- Build-vs-buy calculus for AI governance (systemprompt mentioned as context, not pitch)

**Bottom of funnel (10%)**: Direct product
- Reference implementations and partnerships
- Specific governance capabilities solving real problems
- Enterprise deployment stories (only with permission, never fabricated)

## Blog Articles

### Types
1. **AI governance guides**: Practical content targeting CTO search queries about controlling AI usage
2. **Build-vs-buy analysis**: Deep dives on why in-house AI governance becomes a maintenance burden
3. **Industry observations**: Contrarian takes on AI adoption backed by real experience
4. **Product updates**: What is new, why it matters for governance, how to use it

### Structure
- **Title**: Clear, includes primary keyword, under 60 characters
- **Introduction** (100 to 150 words): Hook, state what you will cover, primary keyword
- **Body** (3 to 5 sections with H2 subheadings): One core idea per section, evidence and examples
- **Conclusion** (75 to 100 words): Takeaways, CTA
- **Meta description**: Under 160 characters, includes primary keyword

### SEO Integration
- One primary keyword, 2 to 3 secondary keywords per piece
- Primary keyword in: title, first paragraph, one subheading, meta description, URL slug
- Target AI governance keywords Anthropic's documentation does not cover
- Answer "People Also Ask" questions where relevant
- Internal links to related systemprompt content

## Reddit Posts

### Rules
- Lead with value, never pitch
- Match subreddit tone exactly
- Share genuine experiences building AI governance infrastructure
- Mention systemprompt only if directly relevant and helpful
- Target: r/ClaudeAI, r/artificial, r/SaaS, r/CTO

### Format
- Title: Clear, specific, often a question or observation about AI governance
- Body: Conversational, detailed, shows real experience
- Always contribute to the discussion, never just promote

## Email Content

### Rules
- From Edward personally
- Short, conversational
- One clear action per email
- Subject lines under 50 characters
- Plain text with minimal formatting

### Types
- Enterprise outreach (introduce systemprompt to CTOs, focus on governance pain)
- Product updates (what is new, why it matters for governance)
- Onboarding (welcome, first steps, quick wins)
- Partner nurture (white-label opportunity education)

## Documentation

### Style
- Clear, patient, step-by-step
- Assume the reader is technically competent (CTO audience)
- Show, do not just tell (include examples and screenshots)
- Every page should answer one specific governance question

## Landing Pages

### Structure
- **Headline**: Primary governance benefit in under 10 words
- **Subheadline**: Elaborates with audience-specific supporting context
- **Hero**: Headline + subheadline + primary CTA + visual
- **Value props**: 3 to 4 governance-focused benefit sections
- **Social proof**: Real testimonials and logos only (never fabricated)
- **FAQ**: Address real objections (build-vs-buy, security, deployment)
- **Final CTA**: Repeat primary action

### CTA Best Practices
- Action verbs: "Schedule a demo", "See the platform", "Start free"
- Reduce friction: "No credit card", "Free tier available", "15-minute setup call"
- Enterprise CTAs should lead to conversation, not self-serve signup
